EAST AFRICAN PILAU

The East African Pilau is a traditional, beautiful fragrant rice dish made with many aromatic spices that adds an amazing depth of flavor to the rice

IINGRIDIENTS

PILAU MASALA - PILAU SPICES

  • 1 stick cinnamon
  • 12 pods cardamon
  • 1 tablespoon cloves
  • 2 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon black peppercorn seeds

RICE PILAU

  • salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 beef stock cubes
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 roma tomatoes diced
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 lb beef sirloin cubed
  • 1 tablespoon pilau masala
  • 2 tablespoon ginger minced
  • 1 serrano chile finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro roughly chopped
  • 2 red onions medium size thinly sliced
  • 5 potatoes medium size peeled and cut into quarters

INSTRUCTIONS

MAKE THE PILAU MASALA

  1. Place the cumin, black pepper, cinnamon and cloves in a dry pan
  2. Roast over low heat until warmed through and fragrant and blend until smooth
  3. You just need 1 to 11 2 tablespoons for the recipe depending on how fragrant you want the rice to be

MAKE THE RICE PILAU

  1. Heat a stockpot on medium high heat. Add oil and heat hot but not smoking
  2. Add the sliced onions and fry for 10 to 15 minutes or till they turn golden brown
  3. Add the garlic, ginger, serrano pepper, cook for 1 to 2 munutes unil fragrant
  4. Add the cubed beef, pilau masala, beef stock cubes, bay leaves, cilantro and salt.
  5. Cook until the meat browns and caramelizes stirring occassionaly for about 8 to 10 minutes
  6. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 4 to 5 minutes until the tomatoes release their liquid
  7. Stir in the potatoes and the water. Bring to a boil and cook for 10 minutes
  8. Stir in the rice and cover lightly with a foil paper. Reduce the heat to low and cook for 20 minutes
  9. Once the rice is cooked and dry, remove the foil paper
  10. Fluff with a fork to ensure the seasonionng are well distributed all around the rice
  11. Serve hot with fresh kachumbari
